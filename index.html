About Us

At ARCADE.ai, we are committed to revamping the old classics with the help of Machine Learning. The aesthitecs of our games tease that sensation of nostalgia of the past, while the controls are completely overidden by the power of ML5. Our aim is to bring players as close as possible to the perception of "controlling the game with your minds". We want to avoid the whole "inserting a micro chip in the brain" part, so the best way to reach our goal is by ditching the controller/keyboard. We do things differently here at ARCADE.ai. Try telling your ninja when to jump, command your bird to flap with a gesture, or break some bricks with a flick of your head. ENJOY !!!

Ninja Run Game

We first trained google's teachable machine to recognize voice commands, and then incorporated that to control an avatar on screen to jump over obstacles.

Flappy Bird Game

This time, we trained the model on pose recognition to make the flappy bird flap on screen. To make the model more responsive, we used one distinct pose and a neutral position to represent any pose which is not the desired pose.

Breakout Game

We trained the model on poses to specifically recognice the nose on the face. We then used this to play the famous breakout game.

Reflection on Project

What inspired you to make this work?

The abilities of ml5 were really impressive to all of us and the first thing that came to mind was the similarity it had to classic gaming consoles like nintendo wii and xbox kinect. It was amazing seeing how technology and AI advancements have brought those expensive and bulky camera systems onto just a laptop with a tiny webcam. Reminiscent of the gaming consoles, ML5 pushed us to experiment the new technology on games.

How does the content of your work relate to some of the concepts we've covered in class?

The project adds a fun play to the work we have done in class. We have learnt about teaching ai and ml5 js and we have taken those skills to develop our game controls.

What tools did you use? How did the tools affect your creative process? (If you found the tools limiting you can also comment on that.)

We used the ml5.js web editor and teachable machine to run and develop the controls for the arcade games. Python http hosting was also used to display the game files in the browser.
The tools are very user-friendly and allow the user a lot of room for creativity. In particular, the teachable machine was a great tool to let your creative process flourish. The platform allows you to experiment a lot with an easy to use interface. In our projects, we were overriding the basic keyboard controls that these games would use, so coming up with a new standard of control in these games needed some imagination. A lot of trial and error went into finding efficient, easy to register, and fun ways to play a game.
We were pretty satisfied with the tools we used, but the most limiting one was the ml5 js editor. As with any coding platform, we frequently ran into errors and lengthy debugging sessions.

What Parts of this Project did not Work Very Well In Your Opinion?What have you learned as a result of making this work? What surprised you?

Other than experience in using these tools, we are left with a sense of awe for the technology itself. When you think about it, this technology is so close to that in sci-fi movies from even a couple years back. Add a holographic screen to the mix and we could be living in our sci-fi fantasies where you can swipe across screens suspended in mid air.
What surprised us the most honestly was how accurate, (and free) some of these tools are. Simple yet sophisticated is a perfect way to describe a teachable machine which we were all really impressed by. What surprised us even more however is that there isn't any mainstream attention to these amazing softwares.

Key Take-aways from the Project?

There is great potential for collaboration between AI and humans especially in the area of music composition, and this project goes to prove that.
